Live-Service Games Worth Your Time

Which ongoing games justify their updates, events, battle passes, and long-term commitment?

Live ServiceMay 13, 2026198+ words

Live-service games ask for more than money. They ask for routine attention. The best ones repay that attention with meaningful updates, fair monetization, strong communities, and systems that remain fresh.

#5 Call of Duty: Warzone

The gunplay is still strong, but the cluttered identity and update burden mean it is best for players already invested in Call of Duty.

Read my Call of Duty: Warzone review

#4 Apex Legends

Excellent movement and gunplay keep Apex relevant even when seasonal quality fluctuates. It is demanding, but mechanically superb.

Read my Apex Legends review

#3 Fortnite

Fortnite remains the strongest entertainment platform in games, with events, modes, and crossovers that constantly refresh the experience.

Read my Fortnite review

#2 World of Warcraft

Two decades of content make WoW intimidating, but its current structure gives committed players a huge range of goals.

Read my World of Warcraft review

#1 Final Fantasy XIV

FFXIV is the rare ongoing game where story, community, and long-term structure matter as much as endgame repetition.

Read my Final Fantasy XIV review

Bottom line:

The key live-service question is simple: does the game respect the player who takes a break? If the answer is no, be careful.
