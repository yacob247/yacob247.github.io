The Best PC Games Under $30 Right Now

Great games don't need triple-A budgets. These are the best experiences you can buy for under $30 — each one delivering more value per dollar than most full-price releases.

DealsMarch 20, 2025377+ words

Budget gaming is having a moment. A wave of indie and small-studio releases have proven that game design creativity and mechanical ambition don't require a $200 million production budget. Here are five games all priced under $30 that compete directly with — and often beat — games costing twice as much.

#5 Outlast (~$20)

The night vision battery mechanic is one of horror gaming's cleverest systems, and the atmosphere in Mount Massive Asylum is genuinely oppressive. For $20, it's one of the most effective horror experiences available. Frequently on sale for under $5.

Read my Outlast (~$20) review

#4 Valheim (~$20)

A 5-person indie team created one of the most satisfying survival games ever made — 10 million copies in two weeks says everything. The building system is a genre standout, and cooperative multiplayer for up to 10 players makes base construction genuinely epic.

Read my Valheim (~$20) review

#3 Lethal Company (~$10)

At $10, this is the most cost-efficient purchase in modern gaming. Proximity voice chat, genuinely creative creature design, and an emergent comedy that makes it the most-watched co-op game of 2023. One solo developer built something that embarrassed studios with 100x the budget.

Read my Lethal Company (~$10) review

#2 RimWorld (~$35 — just above budget, but worth it)

Technically just above $30, but the replayability per dollar is unmatched in all of gaming. RimWorld is a story generator — no two runs are the same, the AI Storyteller creates genuinely surprising narratives, and the modding community has produced years of additional content. It's the best value in the genre.

Read my RimWorld (~$35 — just above budget, but worth it) review

#1 Minecraft (~$27)

The best-selling game in history at well under $30 — and still receiving free, high-quality updates 15 years later. No paid DLC for core content. Infinite procedural worlds. The Java Edition modding community is one of gaming's richest ecosystems. Nothing else at this price offers this much content or creative freedom.

Read my Minecraft (~$27) review

Bottom line:

If you can only pick one: Minecraft for creative freedom, Lethal Company for co-op fun, RimWorld for solo depth. All of these are regularly on sale — set a price alert and you can often get them for half these prices.
