How Visual Spectrogram Editing Works: A Deep Dive

Unlike traditional vocal removers that rely on simple out-of-phase stereo cancellation (which damages spatial stereo signals and wipes out drum transients panned dead-center), our Manual Spectrogram Eraser uses multi-dimensional Short-Time Fourier Transforms (STFT) to convert raw, time-domain audio wave structures into visually identifiable frequency spectra.

Digital Signal Processing (DSP) & Fourier Mathematics

The Mathematics of the Short-Time Fourier Transform (STFT)

To convert a continuous time-varying audio signal $x(t)$ into its mathematical spectrogram layout, we break down the signal into finite, overlapping windows. Each localized window undergoes a discrete Fourier transform computation:

STFT{x[n]}(m, w) = ∑ [ x[n] · w[n - m] · e^(-j · w · n) ]

Auto-Detect Heuristics & Filtering

The Auto-Detect (Beta) feature utilizes a localized offline audio context to apply a bandpass biquad filter focusing exclusively on fundamental vocal formants ($300\text{Hz}$ to $3000\text{Hz}$). It calculates Root Mean Square (RMS) energy densities at $1\text{fps}$ granularity to map potential vocal regions.
